History & Description

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Constructed between 1632 and 1648, it stands as a symbol of eternal love and one of the most iconic monuments in the world.

The monument is renowned for its symmetrical design and intricate craftsmanship. The main structure is made of white marble and adorned with precious stones. It features a large central dome surrounded by four smaller domes, all supported by a massive central structure.

The Taj Mahal is widely considered one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. It has inspired countless artists and architects throughout history and remains a symbol of love and devotion. In 1983, the Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its outstanding universal value. It attracts millions of visitors each year who come to admire its beauty and learn about its rich history.
